Human Centered Design Approach for T-Mobile Bill Payments

Human centered design | Behavioral Economics

During my design fellowship at Future Cognition Collaborative 2018 in San Francisco, our team worked with T-Mobile, a leading mobile service provider in USA to redesign their late-payment collection process, and ultimately improve their overall bill payment customer experience. Here we incorporated a human centered design approach and conducted a series of interviews, collaborative ideation sessions and co-creation sessions with multiple stakeholders.

The Challenge

How might we make T-Mobile's late-payment collection process painless, and overall bill payment experience joyful and rewarding?

T-Mobile has a bill payment problem: every month, nearly 40% of its nearly 75 million customers are late on their bill—that’s a $700 million for T-Mobile every year. We started from generalized brief around T-mobile's late payer problem: "What is the best way to empower T-Mobile customers to enhance their financial lives by leveraging wireless and other data to learn about, anticipate, and acquire products and services that meet their future financial needs?"

The Outcome

Loyal-T, a new payment rewards platform that rewards customers for good financial behaviors. It leverages T-Mobile’s wireless data to provide contextual offers to customers in support of their goals.

We shared with T-Mobile finished designs and assets to help them generate internal excitment and buy-in to launch the loyal-T platform. In addition, we delivered a comprehensive report of insights into the existing bill payment and late-payment collection customer experience including several other directional concepts to enhance overall bill payment experience including design solutions to force sustainable behavior changes into late payers.

The Process

We recruited late payers through Craigslist and conducted semi structured interviewes with them. We used several "sacrificial concepts" to seed the discussion and trigger reactions from late payers.

By conducting in-person interviews with a total of 13 wireless customers including 6 extended interviews with late payers (60-90 mins) and 7 short interviews with on-time payers, we were able to generate key insights and get feedback on our initial concepts. These insights mainly focus on people’s perceptions around their finances and usage of data.

We realized that not all late-payers are created equal; by classifying them and giving tailored offers, T-Mobile can increase on-time payments.

Based on these insights, we generated hundreds of ideas around how we might enhance customer loyalty using T-Mobile’s vast network and wireless data.
